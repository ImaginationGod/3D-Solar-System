Solar System

The solar system is a vast and dynamic collection of celestial bodies bound together by gravity, with the Sun at its center. The Sun, a medium-sized star composed mostly of hydrogen and helium, provides the energy that drives processes on the planets and sustains life on Earth. Its immense gravitational pull keeps the planets, moons, asteroids, and comets in orbit around it, forming a complex and ever-evolving system. There are eight major planets in the solar system, arranged in order of their distance from the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. These planets are divided into two main categories: the terrestrial planets (Mercury through Mars), which are rocky and smaller, and the gas giants (Jupiter and Saturn) and ice giants (Uranus and Neptune), which are much larger and composed mainly of gases and ices. Each planet has unique features, such as rings, magnetic fields, and diverse atmospheres.
